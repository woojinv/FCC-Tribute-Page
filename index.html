The Apostle Paul

A servant of Christ Jesus

While on his way to destroy the Christian community at Damascus, Saul was struck by a flash of light and was asked, "Saul, Saul, why are you persecuting me?"

From Saul to the Apostle Paul

Saul is first born in Rome and grows up in Tarsus, studying under the supervision of Gamaliel, one of the most noted teachers of Jewish Law in history.

So devout for the Jewish law was he that with the rise of Christianity, he approves of the stoning of a man named Stephen, a zealous believer of Jesus Christ.

Saul continues his perscution, dragging off men and women of the church to prison.

He decides specifically to target the believers at Damascus, but on his way there, he is born again.

He has a supernatural experience encountering Jesus, after his crucifixion, and leaves the encounter blind, needing to be led by hand for the remainder of the journey.

A few days later, he recovers his sight, and begins to proclaim Jesus as the Son of God, the very message he stoned Stephen for believing in.

From that point on he begins travelling all around the Mediterranean Sea planting churches and preaching the message of Jesus Christ.

During all this, he suffers imprisonments, countless near-death beatings, nearly 200 lashes to the back, beatings with rods, stoning, shipwrecks, sleepless nights, hunger, thirst, cold, and exposure to the elements.

Finally, Paul is arrested in Jerusalem due to his adversaries inciting the city against him.

He is transported to Caesarea to be judged by Felix the governor, where he remains in prison for the next two years.

He is tried at court once again by his accusers, and he makes an appeal to Caesar, where it is decided that he will be shipped to Rome.

After suffering a shipwreck, he eventually makes it to Rome, but it turns out that no one in Rome actually wants to accuse him or keep him in prison. However, they have heard much about him, and desire to hear what he has to say about Jesus.

For two whole years he continues to preach the gospel of Christianity in Rome and eventually, dies a martyr.

300 years later, Christianity is instituted as the official religion of the Roman Empire.
